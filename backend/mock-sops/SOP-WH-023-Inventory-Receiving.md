# SOP-WH-023: Inventory Receiving & Verification

**Document ID:** SOP-WH-023  
**Version:** 2.5  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Department:** Warehouse Operations  
**Owner:** Receiving Manager  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Classification:** Internal — Receiving & Inventory Team  

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP establishes the procedures for receiving, verifying, and put-away of all inbound shipments at RCCB warehouse facilities. Proper execution ensures inventory accuracy, product quality, and supply chain integrity.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all inbound deliveries including:
- Finished goods from Coca-Cola production facilities
- Raw materials and packaging supplies
- Third-party distributed products
- Returned goods and warranty claims
- Intercompany transfers

## 3. Pre-Receiving Preparation

### 3.1 Daily Preparation
1. Review the daily receiving schedule in WMS (Warehouse Management System)
2. Verify dock door assignments for expected deliveries
3. Ensure receiving equipment is staged: pallet jacks, barcode scanners, thermometers
4. Confirm adequate staging area space
5. Print expected PO (Purchase Order) list for the shift

### 3.2 Dock Area Readiness
- Dock plates/levelers in working condition
- Area clean and free of obstructions
- Adequate lighting at dock doors
- Temperature monitoring devices calibrated (for cold-chain)

## 4. Receiving Procedure

### Step 1: Carrier Check-In
1. Verify carrier identity and appointment against the receiving schedule
2. Assign dock door and direct carrier to staging position
3. Collect and review transportation documents:
   - Bill of Lading (BOL)
   - Packing slip
   - Certificate of Analysis (if applicable)
   - Temperature log (for cold-chain shipments)

### Step 2: Unloading Inspection
1. Inspect trailer condition before unloading:
   - Check for damage, contamination, or pest evidence
   - Verify trailer temperature for refrigerated loads (must be ≤ 40°F / 4°C)
   - Note any seal discrepancies on BOL
2. Begin unloading to receiving staging area
3. Separate damaged goods immediately — do NOT mix with clean product

### Step 3: Quantity Verification
1. Count all cases/units against the PO and BOL
2. For pallet loads: verify pallet count and cases-per-pallet (spot-check 10% minimum)
3. Record actual quantities received in WMS
4. **Discrepancy threshold: 2%**
   - ≤ 2% discrepancy: note on BOL, process normally
   - > 2% discrepancy: escalate to Receiving Supervisor immediately
   - Document all discrepancies with photos

### Step 4: Quality Verification
1. Check lot codes and expiration dates on product
2. Verify product matches PO specifications (SKU, description, packaging)
3. Inspect for visible damage: dents, leaks, crushed packaging
4. For cold-chain products:
   - Record product temperature with calibrated probe thermometer
   - Acceptable range: 33°F – 40°F (0.5°C – 4°C)
   - Reject if temperature exceeds 45°F (7°C)

### Step 5: WMS Logging
1. Scan all received items into WMS within **2 hours** of unloading
2. Apply FIFO (First In, First Out) date labels to every pallet
3. Generate and apply warehouse location labels
4. Record the following in WMS:
   - PO number
   - Actual quantities received
   - Lot codes and expiration dates
   - Receiving dock door and timestamp
   - Carrier name and BOL number
   - Any noted discrepancies or quality issues

### Step 6: Put-Away
1. Follow WMS-directed put-away locations
2. Confirm placement with barcode scan at the storage location
3. Ensure proper product segregation:
   - Allergens separated from non-allergens
   - Chemicals separated from food products
   - Temperature-sensitive items in appropriate zones
4. Stack pallets per zone guidelines (max heights posted at each zone)
5. Complete put-away confirmation in WMS

## 5. Handling Discrepancies

### 5.1 Quantity Short
- Note shortage on BOL (driver signs acknowledgment)
- Create shortage claim in WMS within 24 hours
- Notify Procurement team
- File carrier claim if applicable

### 5.2 Quantity Over
- Accept overage up to 5% of PO quantity
- Overage > 5%: contact Procurement for authorization
- Log all overages in WMS with overage reason code

### 5.3 Damaged Goods
- Segregate in designated quarantine area
- Photograph damage and upload to WMS damage record
- File damage claim within 48 hours
- Do NOT return damaged goods to carrier without Receiving Supervisor approval

### 5.4 Temperature Excursion (Cold-Chain)
- Reject shipment if core temperature > 45°F (7°C)
- For borderline cases (40°F – 45°F): hold in quarantine pending QA review
- Document temperature readings with calibrated device
- Notify Quality Assurance team immediately
