## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the operational and safety requirements for all powered industrial truck (forklift) operations at Contoso warehouse facilities. All forklift operators must read, understand, and comply with this procedure.

## 2. Scope

Covers all forklift types used at Contoso facilities:
- Sit-down counterbalance (electric and propane)
- Stand-up reach trucks
- Order pickers
- Pallet jacks (powered)
- Turret trucks

## 3. Operator Certification Requirements

### 3.1 Initial Certification
- Complete Contoso Forklift Training Program (16 hours classroom + practical)
- Pass written exam (minimum 80% score)
- Pass practical driving evaluation with certified trainer
- Receive operator certification card (valid for 3 years)

### 3.2 Recertification
- Annual refresher training (4 hours)
- Recertification evaluation every 3 years
- Immediate retraining required after any incident or near-miss

### 3.3 Disqualification Events
- Operating under the influence of drugs/alcohol
- Reckless operation causing injury or property damage
- Failure to complete annual refresher training
- Three or more safety violations within 12 months

## 4. Pre-Shift Inspection (Contoso Form FL-101)

Every operator must complete Form FL-101 **before each shift**:

### 4.1 Visual Inspection
- [ ] Tires — no excessive wear, cuts, or flat spots
- [ ] Forks — no cracks, bends, or excessive wear
- [ ] Mast and chains — no visible damage or excessive wear
- [ ] Fluid levels — hydraulic, engine oil, coolant (propane units)
- [ ] Battery — charge level adequate, no corrosion on terminals
- [ ] Safety labels — all warning labels legible

### 4.2 Operational Check
- [ ] Brakes — service brake and parking brake functional
- [ ] Steering — responsive, no excessive play
- [ ] Horn — audible from 50 feet minimum
- [ ] Lights — headlights, tail lights, and strobe operational
- [ ] Backup alarm — audible
- [ ] Hydraulic controls — lift, tilt, side shift smooth and responsive
- [ ] Seatbelt — functional and properly adjusted

### 4.3 Deficiency Reporting
- Any deficiency found → Tag unit **OUT OF SERVICE** immediately
- Complete Contoso Equipment Deficiency Report (Form WH-EDR-200)
- Notify maintenance team and shift supervisor
- Do NOT operate a deficient forklift under any circumstances

## 5. Operating Rules

### 5.1 Speed Limits
| Zone | Maximum Speed |
|------|--------------|
| General warehouse | 5 mph |
| Pedestrian zones | 3 mph |
| Loading docks | 3 mph |
| Intersections/blind corners | 3 mph |
| Ramp areas | 3 mph |
| Cold storage | 3 mph |

### 5.2 Travel Rules
- Always travel with forks lowered (4–6 inches above floor)
- Travel with load tilted back against the mast
- Sound horn at ALL blind corners, intersections, and doorways
- Maintain 3 truck-lengths following distance
- Never drive with forks elevated
- Yield to pedestrians at all times
- Use designated forklift travel lanes

### 5.3 Load Handling
- Never exceed the rated load capacity (posted on data plate)
- Center the load on forks before lifting
- Tilt mast back to stabilize load before travel
- Stack pallets squarely — maximum 3-high for standard pallets
- Use load backrest extension for unstable loads
- Never use forks to push or pull other equipment

### 5.4 Parking Procedure
1. Park in designated forklift parking area
2. Lower forks fully to the floor
3. Tilt mast forward until forks are flat
4. Set parking brake
5. Turn off ignition and remove key
6. For electric units: plug in for charging if below 30%

## 6. Pedestrian Safety

- Pedestrians ALWAYS have the right of way
- Maintain eye contact with pedestrians before proceeding
- Use horn and flashing lights in pedestrian-dense areas
- Never allow riders on the forklift (no passengers)
- Pedestrians must stay outside the 6-foot forklift exclusion zone

## 7. Charging and Refueling

### 7.1 Electric Forklifts
- Charge only in designated charging areas with proper ventilation
- Wear PPE: safety glasses, rubber gloves, face shield (for battery maintenance)
- Check water levels weekly; add distilled water only
- Never smoke or use open flames near charging areas

### 7.2 Propane Forklifts
- Exchange tanks outdoors or in well-ventilated areas only
- Inspect tank, hose, and fittings before connecting
- Secure tank with retaining bracket
- Report any gas odor immediately

## 8. Incident Reporting

Any forklift-related incident must be reported immediately:
1. Stop the forklift and secure the area
2. Check for injuries — call 911 if needed
3. Notify shift supervisor within 5 minutes
4. Complete Contoso Incident Report (Form WH-IR-300) within 24 hours
5. Forklift must be inspected before returning to service
