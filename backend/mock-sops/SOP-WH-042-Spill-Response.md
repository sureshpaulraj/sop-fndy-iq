# SOP-WH-042: Warehouse Spill Response & Cleanup

**Document ID:** SOP-WH-042  
**Version:** 3.1  
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2026  
**Department:** Warehouse Operations  
**Owner:** Safety & Compliance Manager  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  
**Classification:** Internal — All Warehouse Employees

## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the required steps for responding to and cleaning up chemical, liquid, or hazardous material spills within Contoso warehouse facilities. Compliance is mandatory for all warehouse personnel.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all Contoso warehouse locations, including:
- Receiving docks
- Storage areas
- Pick/pack zones
- Shipping docks
- Refrigerated storage areas

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Minor Spill** | Less than 5 gallons of non-hazardous material |
| **Major Spill** | 5+ gallons OR any hazardous material |
| **EHS** | Environmental Health & Safety |
| **PPE** | Personal Protective Equipment |
| **Spill Kit** | Pre-assembled containment and cleanup supplies |

## 4. Immediate Response (Within 5 Minutes)

### 4.1 Discovery Actions
1. **STOP** — Cease all operations in the immediate area
2. **ASSESS** — Identify the spilled substance (check container labels or SDS)
3. **CORDON** — Place caution cones and spill barriers around the affected area
4. **NOTIFY** — Alert the shift supervisor and EHS team immediately via radio or phone

### 4.2 Personnel Safety
- Evacuate non-essential personnel from a 25-foot radius
- Do NOT attempt cleanup without proper PPE
- If substance is unknown, treat as hazardous until identified

## 5. Minor Spill Cleanup Procedure

1. Don appropriate PPE: nitrile gloves, safety glasses, chemical-resistant apron
2. Retrieve the nearest spill kit (locations marked on facility maps)
3. Apply absorbent materials (pads, booms, or granular absorbent)
4. Work from the outside edge inward to prevent spreading
5. Collect all contaminated materials in designated disposal bags
6. Clean the area with appropriate neutralizing solution
7. Verify the area is dry and safe before resuming operations

## 6. Major Spill Response

1. Activate the Facility Emergency Response Plan (SOP-EHS-001)
2. Contact the EHS Hotline: **1-800-Contoso-EHS**
3. Evacuate all personnel from the affected zone
4. Shut down HVAC in the affected area (for volatile substances)
5. Do NOT attempt cleanup — wait for the Hazmat Response Team
6. Secure all exits and ventilation points
7. Provide SDS information to first responders

## 7. Documentation Requirements

All spills must be documented within **24 hours** using the Contoso EHS Incident Report Form (Form EHS-IR-100):

- Date, time, and location of the spill
- Substance name, quantity, and SDS reference
- Root cause (if known)
- Personnel involved and any injuries
- Cleanup actions taken
- Corrective actions to prevent recurrence
- Photographs of the scene (before and after cleanup)

## 9. Training Requirements

- All warehouse employees: Annual spill awareness training
- Shift supervisors: Spill Response Coordinator certification (every 2 years)
- Hazmat team members: 40-hour HAZWOPER initial + 8-hour annual refresher
